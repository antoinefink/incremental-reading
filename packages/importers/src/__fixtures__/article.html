The Spacing Effect

By Hermann Ebbinghaus

Spaced repetition exploits the spacing effect: information is retained far better when study sessions are distributed over time rather than crammed into a single block.

Why intervals grow

After each successful recall the optimal interval lengthens, because the memory trace has been reconsolidated and decays more slowly. A good scheduler therefore increases the gap geometrically while a card is answered correctly.

The classic forgetting curve, first measured by Ebbinghaus in 1885, shows that retention falls off exponentially without reinforcement. Reviewing just before the predicted forgetting point flattens that curve at minimal cost.
